Before

Hurricanes can be tracked one to three days ahead of the impact and sometimes even more. That gives us time to be prepared for a potential evacuation. We also need to remember that this kind of storms can change directions and even speed that why we also need to pay atention to the news, and stay INFORMED!

Here we will give some basic knowledge about the steps we should take before a hurricane impacts

    Prepare

  • Have a Disaster Supply Kit – This can be of great help not only during a hurricane
  • Where To Go? – A hurricane can hit everytime so you need to know what are good shelters near you in case of an emergency
  • Evacuation Route – In case somethin happens you should be able to know what routes you can take in order to escape the impact zone
  • Physical Maps - Do not rely on electronic devices, networks may be down during the storm

    If You Need To Evacuate

    You should at least have the following items

  • Food, drinking water, snacks
  • Prescription and emergency medicines
  • Personal hygiene items
  • Extra clothing, pillows, blankets
  • Cash

Terminology

  • Tropical Storm Watch - Tropical storm conditions are possible, usually within 48 hours.
  • Tropical Storm Warning - Tropical storm conditions are expected, usually within 36 hours.
  • Hurricane Watch - Hurricane conditions are possible, usually within 48 hours.
  • Hurricane Warning - Hurricane conditions are expected, usually within 36 hours.
  • Evacuation Order - Imminent threat to life and property exists. Individuals MUST relocate and seek refuge in an inland, non-evacuation area.

      • Transportation

      County transportation facilities and services shut down three hours before hurricane or tropical storm winds reach a sustained 39 miles per hour.

      As you prepare for hurricane season, don't forget to secure your boat. Also remember that you should never try and ride out a hurricane in your boat. Once evacuations have started off the barrier islands, Intercoastal Waterway bridges will only be opened infrequently until lockdown.

    During a Hurricane

    Remember to always check your radio, television, or any social media avaliable for weather updates and instructions drom public safety oficials.

      Hurricane Watch Announced

    • Fill up your vehicle tank
    • Get Extra Cash
    • Bring in loose objects from outside

      Hurricane Warning Announced

    • Secure all windows
    • Place important paper in a waterproof container and store them in the highest floor of your home

      If you have to evacuate

    • Follow all the directions from the officials
    • Bring Emergency supplies, food, medicines, water, blankets, phones radios and batteries
    • Unplug appliances, turn off utilities such as electricity and the main water valve.
    • Lock windows and doors at your home
    • Bring extra cash

      If you don't have to evacuate

    • Stay in a room with no windows
    • If flooding threatens your home, turn off the electricity at the main breaker
    • do not go outside during the eye of the storm!
    • Use flashlights, not candles or kerosene lamps, as your light source.
    • Clean your bathtub with bleach and fill it with water for washing and flushing
    • Set your refrigerator to maximum cold and keep it closed.

    After

    More deaths occur after a hurricane than during. These deaths come from people being too anxious to get outside and survey the damage where they come into contact with downed power lines or unstable trees, etc.

      You can follow this safety steps after a hurricane

    • Remain indoors while local authorities say is safe to go outside
    • Do not operate charcoal grills, propane camping stoves or generators indoors
    • Continue to check the news by radio or tv
    • Be patient and careful. Cleanup after a storm can take time.
    • Call police or utility companies immediately to report hazards such as downed power lines, broken gas or water mains, overturned gas tanks, etc.
    • Watch for weakened roads, bridges, tree limbs or porches which could collapse unexpectedly.
